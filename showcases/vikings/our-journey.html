The Vikings Story

Built on teamwork, effort, and accountability.

Nov 2021

The Club is Founded

Phil Molyneux — a former National League player with international experience in America and Hong Kong — launches the first Stortford Vikings training session with 30 enthusiastic children. His mission: bring basketball to Bishop's Stortford and build something the community could be proud of.

Early 2022

Rapid Growth

Word spreads fast. Within months the club outgrows its original capacity. Community sessions for ages 7–14 are structured into age groups, and a coaching team begins to form around Phil's vision — Kassim Chohan, Rob London, Jo Brown, and Alex Riley join the staff.

Mid 2022

250 Members in 9 Months

By the summer of 2022, the Vikings have over 250 registered members. Sessions are capped at 30 players and run in 6–7 week blocks. The club establishes its home at Birchwood High School.

2022–23

The Valkyries Launch

Recognising the need for girls-only basketball, the Valkyries programme is launched — providing a welcoming space for girls aged 7+ to develop skills, build confidence, and compete. The response is overwhelming.

2023–24

Competitive Squads & Fixtures

The Vikings enter local and regional leagues with squad-level teams. Trial selection, competitive fixtures, and structured strength & conditioning programmes raise the bar. The club builds a reputation for strong team spirit and competitive play.

2025–26

Where We Are Today

Today the club runs Community Sessions (ages 7–14), Squad Programmes (ages 10+), and Valkyries (girls 7+) with multiple age groups, regular fixtures, and a growing family of players, parents, and supporters. We're just getting started.

The Future

What's Next

More age groups, more fixtures, more community events, and ultimately putting Bishop's Stortford on the basketball map. Our ambition is to grow the game locally and create pathways for every player — from first touch to competitive level.
